I am a graphic designer and novice type designer. I graduated from the Rhode Island School of Design with a BFA in Graphic Design in 2014. From August 2014 to May 2015 I was a Fulbright-Nehru Student Researcher studying type design for Devanagari at the Industrial Design Center at IIT-Mumbai.

I've worked on projects for print and web, but I've also organized events, lead people, and written stuff.

I'm enchanted by typographic forms and compelled by the implications of typography as a vessel for communication. I'm curious about political and ethical questions around typography, particularly for non-latin scripts in the digital age.

My work has been published in Filipino Folk Foundry by Hardworking Goodlooking and for / with / in by The Design Office.

During my time at RISD I was the president of RISD STEAM, a student group founded by Sarah Pease seeking to reimagine the role of the artist in society via combining Art with STEM. I'm proud to say the organizaton has grown to include groups at Brown, MIT, and Yale.

I speak Spanish and Hindi conversationally, and French fluently.
